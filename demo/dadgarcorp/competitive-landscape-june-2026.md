# Competitive Landscape — June 2026

**Owner:** Product Marketing (Theo Lindqvist) · Reviewed with sales leadership June 19

## The field

We compete in three directions at once: open-source DIY (Backstage), venture-backed IDP startups (Corteza, EnvoyDeck), and "good enough" bundles from the clouds.

### Backstage (open source, CNCF)
The default alternative in every enterprise deal. Free to adopt, expensive to live with — customers report 3–5 platform engineers just to keep it running.

- **Where they win:** unlimited customization, no procurement, CNCF halo, existing plugin investment.
- **Where we win:** time-to-value (days vs. quarters), environments as a first-class object (Backstage catalogs services; it doesn't *provision* anything), and total cost once headcount is counted honestly.
- **Watch:** Brightbeam churned to a DIY Backstage build in June — but the exit interview said the real driver was our missing SSO, not Backstage's strengths.

### Corteza ("the developer portal company")
Closest direct competitor. Strong design, loud marketing, Series C in February ($85M).

- **Where they win:** slicker service catalog UI, scorecard/gamification features PMs love in demos, faster to say yes on one-off features.
- **Where we win:** actual infrastructure provisioning (they orchestrate tickets; we replace them), Terraform-native depth, policy guardrails.
- **Pricing intel:** list at $35/dev/mo flat; discounts to ~$25 at 500 seats. No environment automation at any tier.

### EnvoyDeck
Ephemeral-environments specialist. Narrow but genuinely fast at PR preview environments.

- **Where they win:** PR-preview workflow is best-in-class; strong bottom-up adoption with frontend teams.
- **Where we win:** persistent and regulated environments, audit, catalog breadth. They're a feature; we're a platform. (Careful saying that in front of customers who love them.)

### Cloud-native bundles (AWS Proton, Azure Deployment Environments)
Free-ish with the cloud bill. Single-cloud by design.

- **Where we win:** every multi-cloud or on-prem-hybrid account, which is most of our enterprise pipeline.

## Positioning guidance for 2.0

Lead with **"the compliant way is the fast way."** Blueprints + guardrails + evidence bundle is a story none of the four can tell end-to-end. Do not lead with catalog features — that's Corteza's home turf.

## Deal risk to monitor

Corteza announced "Environments (beta)" at their user conference June 11. Sales has not yet lost a deal to it, but two prospects asked. Assume they reach parity marketing (not parity product) by our launch week.
